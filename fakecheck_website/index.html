How Our AI Detects Scams

Behind the scenes of the FakeCheck processing pipeline.

01

Data Extraction

OCR models extract all text from the poster. NLP identifies key entities like company name, stipend, and contact info.

02

Feature Analysis

BERT-based models analyze semantic meaning, looking for urgent language, vague requirements, or unrealistic promises.

03

Visual Inspection

CNNs analyze the design patterns, looking for low-quality logos, mismatched fonts, or generic stock templates common in scams.

04

Final Verdict

A Random Forest classifier combines all signals to generate a final confidence score and highlight specific red flags.

Common Fake Internship Red Flags

Learn what our AI looks for when analyzing a poster.

"Too Good to Be True" Stipend

Offering unusually high pay for tasks requiring zero specific skills or prior experience.

No Official Website

Applications that direct to a Google Form instead of a verifiable company career portal.

Sketchy Contact Info

Using personal email addresses (Gmail, Yahoo) instead of a corporate domain for applications.

Fake Urgency

"Hiring immediately", "Act fast", or pressure tactics to make you apply without thinking.

Asking for Money

Any request for a "training fee", "registration fee", or equipment purchase upfront.

Vague Job Description

Roles that don't clearly explain the day-to-day duties or required technical qualifications.
